Why this matters

BS has closed-form Greeks. Heston doesn't — finite differences are the only option. The error analysis shows the V-shaped curve: too large h gives truncation error (O(h²) for central diff), too small h gives floating-point cancellation error. Optimal h ≈ √ε · S where ε ≈ 2.2 × 10⁻¹⁶. Comparing BS analytical vs FD validates the numerical method; then the same method applied to Heston gives Greeks that can't be obtained any other way.
