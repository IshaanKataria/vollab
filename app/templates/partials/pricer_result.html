Why the prices differ

BS assumes constant volatility σ. Heston models volatility as a stochastic process with mean reversion (κ), long-run level (θ), vol-of-vol (ξ), and spot-vol correlation (ρ). When ρ < 0, Heston produces a left skew — OTM puts are priced higher than BS predicts.
